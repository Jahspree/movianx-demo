## The 9 New Worlds — Strict Emotional Diversity

Each world has a unique emotional signature, visual language, lighting identity, and creator voice. No overlap with existing assets.

### Movie Worlds (3)

**1. The Salt Line**  
- **Type:** Slow-burn coastal folk horror  
- **Creator:** Elara Voss (folklore archivist turned filmmaker)  
- **Emotional Core:** Salt, wind, inherited guilt, the sea as witness. Cold, damp, tactile dread.  
- **Visual Language:** Overcast skies, weathered wood, salt-crusted surfaces, low sun through sea mist, long static shots.  
- **Primary Rail:** folk-horror-coastal  
- **Lighting Identity:** Flat grey daylight + single warm practical in interiors. Almost no shadows at golden hour.

**2. A Brief History of Light**  
- **Type:** Intimate near-future grief sci-fi  
- **Creator:** Dr. Nadia Solen (theoretical physicist who lost her partner)  
- **Emotional Core:** The last images the dead saw. Quiet wonder mixed with unbearable tenderness and loss.  
- **Visual Language:** Soft window light, projected memories on walls, clinical but human lab spaces, heavy use of negative space and breath.  
- **Primary Rail:** intimate-sci-fi-grief  
- **Lighting Identity:** Motivated window light + soft practicals. Very little hard contrast.

**3. The Low Season**  
- **Type:** Neo-noir crime drama  
- **Creator:** Mateo Rivas (former crime photographer)  
- **Emotional Core:** Faded glamour, quiet violence, off-season loneliness in a once-glorious resort town.  
- **Visual Language:** Harsh midday sun on peeling stucco, neon at dusk in empty pools, long shadows in abandoned lobbies, cigarette smoke and sea air.  
- **Primary Rail:** neo-noir-coastal  
- **Lighting Identity:** High contrast Mediterranean sun + sodium vapor + deep shadows.

### Story Worlds (2)

**4. The Archive of Small Mercies**  
- **Type:** Warm magical realist family stories  
- **Creator:** Sister Ana-María Delgado (90-year-old nun who has collected other people's kindnesses for 60 years)  
- **Emotional Core:** Quiet joy, forgotten acts of grace, the sacred in the ordinary.  
- **Visual Language:** Golden afternoon light through lace curtains, handwritten notes, pressed flowers, old radios, children's drawings on kitchen walls.  
- **Primary Rail:** magical-realist-stories  
- **Lighting Identity:** Warm, soft, dappled sunlight. Very little darkness.

**5. Static & Grace**  
- **Type:** Found-audio epistolary psychological thriller  
- **Creator:** "The Operator" (anonymous shortwave archivist)  
- **Emotional Core:** Connection across distance and time through fragile radio signals. Loneliness + unexpected intimacy.  
- **Visual Language:** Dark radio rooms, glowing dials, maps with pins, headphones on tables, night windows with distant city lights, static as texture.  
- **Primary Rail:** audio-thriller-found-media  
- **Lighting Identity:** Low-key practicals from equipment + cool blue night light through windows.

### Music Worlds (2)

**6. The Last Polar Night**  
- **Type:** Arctic ambient / drone sound world  
- **Creator:** Dr. Ingrid Solberg (glaciologist and field recordist)  
- **Emotional Core:** Vast cold silence, the sound of ice breathing, human fragility against geological time.  
- **Visual Language:** Endless white-blue landscapes, research stations half-buried in snow, aurora, frozen equipment, extreme minimalism.  
- **Primary Rail:** polar-ambient-worlds  
- **Lighting Identity:** Blue hour + aurora + very low sun. Almost monochrome.

**7. Highway Reveries (1978)**  
- **Type:** Lost American road movie soundtrack world  
- **Creator:** "The Driver" (anonymous cassette collector)  
- **Emotional Core:** Freedom mixed with quiet melancholy. The road as both escape and trap. Warm analog nostalgia.  
- **Visual Language:** Golden hour on empty highways, motel signs, dusty dashboards, Polaroids on visors, grainy 16mm road footage aesthetic.  
- **Primary Rail:** road-movie-ambient  
- **Lighting Identity:** Late afternoon golden light + dashboard glow + neon motel signs at dusk.

### Creator Worlds (2)

**8. The Glass House Archives**  
- **Type:** Private visual diary of a reclusive cinematographer  
- **Creator:** Lucien Moreau (legendary but reclusive DP who only shoots on 35mm and 16mm)  
- **Emotional Core:** Obsession with light as character. The beauty of decay and passing time.  
- **Visual Language:** Studies of light through glass, abandoned modernist houses, lens flares on dust, texture of film grain, handwritten exposure notes.  
- **Primary Rail:** cinematographer-diaries  
- **Lighting Identity:** Pure natural light studies. No artificial sources in most images.

**9. Memory Palace (After the Fire)**  
- **Type:** Experimental theater director's memory installation world  
- **Creator:** Irina Petrov (director whose family's historic opera house burned down in 1998)  
- **Emotional Core:** Reconstruction of memory through charred remains, costume fragments, and projected ghosts.  
- **Visual Language:** Burned velvet curtains, charred sheet music, half-melted chandeliers, projected old 8mm footage on ruined walls, ash and gold leaf.  
- **Primary Rail:** theater-memory-worlds  
- **Lighting Identity:** Fire-damaged practicals + projected light + deep crimson and gold against black.
