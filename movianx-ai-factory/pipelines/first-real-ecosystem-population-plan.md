## Planned New Worlds (8 Core + Supporting Atmosphere)

Each world has a distinct emotional signature, lighting language, and creator voice. No overlap with existing (The Hollowing Signal, Veil of Static, Weight of Silence, Event Horizon Choir, Salt Line, Last Polar Night, Quiet Frequency).

### 1. Emotional Drama — "The Last Summer We Spoke"
- Creator: Lena Moreau (playwright)
- Emotional core: Quiet reconciliation, memory, the weight of unsaid things between family.
- Visual language: Golden late-summer light, old wooden houses, handwritten letters, long shadows, warm but melancholic.
- Target assets: 4–5 (poster, backdrop, rail, creator banner, intimate still).
- Primary rail: drama-intimate-rails.

### 2. Dreamlike Surrealism — "The Museum of Unfinished Dreams"
- Creator: The Curator (anonymous)
- Emotional core: Abandoned dreams stored as physical objects in an endless museum. Wonder + gentle melancholy.
- Visual language: Soft focus, floating objects, impossible architecture, warm sepia + cool blue mixes, dream logic.
- Target: 4 (strong hero + rail + immersive + banner).
- Primary rail: surreal-dream-worlds.

### 3. Atmospheric Sci-Fi / Wonder — "The Quiet After the Signal"
- Creator: Dr. Amara Kade (xenolinguist)
- Emotional core: First contact happened quietly. Now humanity lives with gentle, strange new presence.
- Visual language: Soft volumetric light, everyday objects touched by alien geometry, vast night skies with one extra star, hopeful + eerie.
- Target: 4–5.
- Primary rail: atmospheric-sci-fi-wonder.

### 4. Immersive Thriller — "The Boundary"
- Creator: Jonah Vale (former border journalist)
- Emotional core: Psychological tension in a divided, liminal city. Paranoia without cheap jumps.
- Visual language: High contrast urban night, fog, reflections in puddles, long empty corridors, red + cold blue.
- Target: 4 (heavy on rail thumbnails + posters).
- Primary rail: thriller-imersive-rails.

### 5. Nostalgic Warmth — "The Record Shop at the End of the World"
- Creator: Marcus "The Needle" Rivera (last record store owner)
- Emotional core: Analog warmth, human connection through music in a fading world. Late 90s / early 2000s soul.
- Visual language: Warm tungsten + golden hour, vinyl textures, handwritten price tags, rainy windows, stacks of records.
- Target: 5 (music + story + creator heavy).
- Primary rail: nostalgic-warmth-worlds.

### 6. Surreal Intimacy — "The Apartment That Remembers"
- Creator: Clara Voss (installation artist)
- Emotional core: An apartment where every object holds the emotional residue of past relationships. Intimate, erotic, melancholic.
- Visual language: Close details of fabric, light through curtains, half-remembered faces in mirrors, soft focus + sharp texture.
- Target: 4.
- Primary rail: surreal-intimacy.

### 7. Ambient Soundscape — "The Underwater Bell"
- Creator: The Hydrophonist (anonymous field recordist)
- Emotional core: Submarine and deep water sound as memory and dream. Pressure, resonance, beauty under the surface.
- Visual language: Deep blues and greens, light rays through water, abandoned structures underwater, bioluminescence.
- Target: 3–4.
- Primary rail: ambient-underwater-worlds.

### 8. Homepage / Pure Atmosphere Assets (no single world)
- 6–8 standalone cinematic atmosphere pieces for hero modules and discovery rails.
- Examples: rainy neon city at night (but warm, not cyberpunk), golden hour empty wheat field with distant train, snow falling on an empty train platform at dusk, etc.
- These fill hero-backgrounds and rail thumbnails heavily.
